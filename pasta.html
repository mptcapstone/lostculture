Pasta

Pasta comes in many shapes, literally.

The most common shapes are usually spaghetti, linguine, and elbow macaroni stocked in grocery aisles. However, there's more than 600 pasta shapes where each are designed to be pair with certain sauces. Since 600 is too much, I'll go through the 5 most common shapes listed below.

Spaghetti

These long thin strands are usually paired with tomato sauces and oil based sauces.

Linguine

Although it looks like fettucine, this is made from flour and water and are typically paired well with lighter sauces like vongole, pesto, or carbonara.

Fettucine

Apart from the linguine, this is made from flour and eggs and are paired well with heavier sauces like cream or bolognese.

Penne

This is a long, narrow shaped tube and is paired well with tuna and heavier sauces. This pasta can also be used for salads, like tuna salads and can be baked (like baked penne).

Rigatoni

Instead of its being narrow like the penne, it's a wider tube that's good with heavier sauces and can even be baked.
