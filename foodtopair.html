Food to Pair

The more, the merrier!

If it's a party of four or a date, these dishes can be used to make a lone meal seem all the more complete. Pasta is normally regarded as an appetizer in the western world, so these are possible accompaniments to that concept. All the food listed below can be paired with each other, as standalones or as a 3-course meal.

  • Pan-Seared Steak
  • Garlic Bread
  • Balsamic and Honey Salad
  • Caprese

Pan-seared Butter Basted Ribeye (Medium Rare)

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 1-2

Ingredients

  • 1.5 inches ribeye
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 2 cloves of garlic, smashed
  • 1 sprig of rosemary
  • salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, add the olive oil and turn on the heat to medium heat. If the oil shimmers, add in your seasoned steak and leave it on one side for 3 minutes. Flip after 3 minutes and leave it on that side for the same amount of time.
  2. For the last two minutes, lower the heat to low and add in the butter, garlic, and rosemary to the pan. Tilt the pan and baste the steak with the butter. Use a meat thermometer and check if the internal temp registers 137~140Â°F and take it off the heat.
  3. Let it rest for 5 minutes before cutting the meat. This will prevent the steak from going dry. Serve with the pan sauce or more salt and pepper if desired.

Garlic Bread

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 4-5

Ingredients

  • 5 tbsp butter
  • 4 cloves of garlic,minced
  • 2 tbsp parsley
  • salt and pepper
  • crusty bread, sourdough or baguette

Instructions

  1. Soften the butter in the microwave before adding the garlic and a tablespoon of parsley. Adjust the taste with salt or pepper.
  2. Use the broiler (top element of oven) to toast the bread. Smear the slices of bread with the desired amount of mixture, then place them onto the top rack on a tray.
  3. Remove from broiler if top side of bread is golden brown and/or toasty. Serve with more parsley or garlic if desired.

Balsamic and Honey Salad

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 5

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp of olive oil
  • 1 tbsp of lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 30g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 30g walnut, crushed
  • 4oz lettuce or mixed greens, chopped
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Make the vinaigrette with olive oil, lemon juice, and salt and pepper. Ready a large bowl and place in your greens with the cherry tomatoes and walnuts.
  2. Pour in the vinaigrette before tossing everything until each green is well coated. Drizzle the balsamic glaze and honey over the salad when serving. Adjust the taste with salt or pepper.

Caprese

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Serves: 5

Ingredients

  • 3 tomatoes, cut into 1/4 inch thick slices
  • 1-2 mounds of mozzarella, cut into 1/4 inch thick slices
  • 20 large basil leaves
  • a drizzle of olive oil
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Layer the tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil alternately until all the ingredients are used up. Adjust the taste with salt and pepper before drizzling the olive oil.
