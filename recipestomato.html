Pomodoro

  • Preparation time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 750g canned tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped basil
  • 500g spaghetti
  • 50g of anchovies
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Ready a pot of boiling water. In a large skillet, add the olive oil and garlic over medium heat. Stir/flip occasionally to prevent the garlic from burning and cook until it is soft. Add the anchovies and stir with the garlic until fragrant. (If minced, add the garlic and anchovies together and stir occasionally until fragrant.)
  2. Pour in the canned tomatoes before stirring. Adjust seasonings with salt. Reduce the heat to low and let simmer with the lid on.
  3. Add a large pinch of salt and spaghetti to the boiling water and cook until al dente. Reserve a half cup of pasta water before adding the pasta to the simmering pot of tomato sauce.
  4. Pour the reserved water into the pot and gently fold the spaghetti with the sauce. Once the sauce coats the pasta, add the chopped basil and cheese before turning the heat off. Plate with more basil or cheese if desired.

Puttanesca

  • Preparation time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1/4 cup of basil, chopped
  • 5 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 25g anchovies
  • 25g capers
  • 6 chopped green olives
  • large pinch of chili flakes
  • 250g canned tomatoes
  • 150g cherry tomatoes
  • 450g penne/linguine
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Ready a pot of boiling water. Pour the olive oil into a large skillet over medium heat and wait until warm before adding the garlic, anchovies, capers, and olives. Stir until fragrant before adding the chili flakes.
  2. Place the cherry tomatoes into the sauce and cover with a lid for five minutes or until the skin blisters. Adjust seasonings to taste. Once it blisters, add the canned tomatoes before stirring and covering again with the lid and heat reduced to low.
  3. While the sauce is cooking, add the pasta and salt to the pot of boiling water and cook until al dente. Once done, reserve a half-cup of pasta water before pouring it into the sauce with the al dente pasta.
  4. Stir the pot until the sauce coats the pasta before turning the heat off. Add the chopped basil before serving.

Bolognese

  • Preparation time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 8-10

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of olive oil
  • 150ml red wine
  • 3 bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 tbsp of tomato paste
  • 1 stock cube
  • 500g rigatoni
  • 500g ground beef
  • 750g canned tomatoes
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Ready a pot of boiling water. In a separate large pot over medium heat, add the olive oil and wait until warm before adding the bell peppers, carrots, and onions. Stir occasionally for 7-10 minutes until the vegetables are soft.
  2. Add the ground beef into the pot and increase the heat to medium-high and cook with vegetables until the ground meat is sizzling/brown all over.
  3. Squeeze the tomato paste and mix with the beef until fragrant/bottom of pan is light brown all over. Before it burns, add in the red wine and stir again until the alcohol has cooked off.
  4. Pour in the canned tomatoes and the stock cube. Stir until everything is well-combined before covering with a lid and reducing the heat back to medium. Simmer for 20-30 minutes or an hour with the heat turned to low.
  5. Add the pasta and salt to the boiling water and cook until perfect. Do not mix with the sauce and serve the pasta with a ladleful of sauce. Garnish with basil and parmesan cheese if desired.
