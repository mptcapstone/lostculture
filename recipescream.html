Carbonara

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking time: 12-15 minutes
  • Serves: 3

Ingredients

  • 3 streaky bacons, chopped
  • 1 egg
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup of pecorino romano
  • 250g of spaghetti
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Ready a pot of boiling water. Crack an egg and crack two more to get the yolks. Mix the egg, egg yolks, and pecorino cheese into a bowl until well-combined. Set aside for later.
  2. Add the pasta and salt to the pot. In a skillet over low heat, add the bacon and render the fat into oil. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Turn off the heat once both are finished cooking. Reserve 1/4 cup of pasta water.
  3. Toss the pasta with the bacon fat before adding the egg mixture with the pasta water. Stir quickly until the sauce thickens or looks creamy. Plate and garnish with pepper if desired.

Truffle Pasta

  • Preparation time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 6

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp of olive oil
  • 1/2 of stock cube
  • 1.5 cups all-purpose cream
  • 4 tbsp black truffle sauce
  • 500g fettucine
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Ready a pot of boiling water. In a large skillet, drizzle olive oil before adding the mushrooms. Cook until mushrooms have shrinked and have caramelized a bit. Reduce the heat to low before pouring in the cream to prevent the cream from separating. Add in the stock cube and truffle sauce and stir until dissolved. Turn off the heat.
  2. Add the pasta and salt to the boiling water, then cook the pasta to al dente. Reserve a half-cup of pasta water before adding the pasta, its water, and butter to the sauce.
  3. Turn on the heat to low before folding the sauce and the pasta. It is finished when the sauce coats the pasta.

Free Rein Cream Pasta

  • Preparation time: 7-10 minutes
  • Cooking time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 5-6

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp of olive oil
  • 1.5 cups of all-purpose cream
  • 1 stock cube
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 350 grams any pasta
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

This recipe can be modified in any way with the addition of meat, seafood, vegetables, spices, and/or cheese hence its name, but it can also be eaten as is.

  1. Ready a pot of boiling water. Dilute the stock cube in a cup of water before adding a drizzle of olive oil and the garlic to a large skillet. Stir until fragrant and add the stock and white wine after. Reduce the sauce to half or until the alcohol has cooked off.
  2. Add the pasta and salt to the boiling water. Cook until al dente then reserve a half-cup of pasta water. Reduce the skillet's heat to low then add in the cream, pasta, and its water to the sauce.
  3. Gently fold in the sauce with the pasta. It is finished when the sauce coats the pasta.
