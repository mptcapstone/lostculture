Essentials

The pantry is your best friend.

These are the bare basics for you to get started with Italian cooking in the kitchen. These can be found in most supermarkets and can work in almost any pasta dish. Listed below are the pantry essentials any cook should have.

The Basics

  • Anchovies
  • Pasta
  • Tomatoes
  • Olive Oil
  • Lemon
  • Basil
  • Garlic

    • Tips! The longer you cook tomatoes or decide to add in salt, they'll become sweeter. A great substitute for salt are the little tins of anchovies since it doesn't adulterate the taste of tomatoes.

    Any kind of citrus should be added at the end of cooking since acids won't be as sour the earlier you add it to any dish.

    I made a recipe using pantry essentials only and can be found here or the oil-based category under recipes.

    Good to Have

  • Cheese
  • White Wine
  • Sardines

    • These aren't entirely necessary, but they're nice to have every once in a while. Cheeses are entirely optional contrary to popular belief. (Please don't hunt me down, Italians.) Good cheeses are hard to come by and are quite expensive, hence its being in this category. White wine is for deglazing the pan fond, but it only gives a very subtle difference in flavor.

    Sardines (or any canned fish like anchovies or tuyo) are great flavor enhancers and are easy to cook with, but again not necessary when you have the pantry essentials already!
